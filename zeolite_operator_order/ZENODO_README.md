## The Core Hypothesis

**ZSM-5 enforces C→K→F→U**: Constraint fires *before* Folding
- 10-ring channel acts as size-selector upstream of reactive sites
- Only small, linear products (ethylene, DEE) survive
- Branching and aromatic intermediates are geometrically forbidden

**MCM-22 executes C→F→K→U**: Folding fires *before* Constraint  
- 12-ring supercage permits free branching chemistry
- Aromatic and bulky intermediates form first
- 10-ring exit then acts as geometric trap (coke precursors)

**Prediction**: This single difference in operator order explains why MCM-22 forms coke readily while ZSM-5 resists it—despite identical acid chemistry.

## Evidence Summary

| Evidence Type | ZSM-5: C→K→F→U | MCM-22: C→F→K→U | Source |
|---|---|---|---|
| **MD Simulation** | 10-ring channel mouth constrains immediately | Benzene intracage only (cannot exit) | Sastre et al. 1999, 2002 |
| **Operando DRIFTS** | Ethoxide first, DEE second | Supercage coke forms before sinusoidal access | Kadam & Shamzhy 2018; Zhou et al. 2019 |
| **Structural Ablation** | Pore-mouth sites drive branching; channel interior suppress it | Boron substitution: moving acid sites into 10-ring suppresses coke | Parmar et al. 2022; MCM-22 boron study 2022 |
| **Classical Theory** | Restricted transition state selectivity (no large molecules) | 12-ring external pockets are open-access | Weisz & Frilette 1960; Lawton et al. 1998 |

**All six evidence types from prior published literature** — not speculative additions.

## Three Falsifiable Predictions

**P1: Temporal DRIFTS Sequence (MCM-22)**  
Contact-time operando DRIFTS should show: ethoxy → diethyl ether → aromatic C₆–C₈ → polymeric coke, in that order. This is the C→F→K→U sequence demonstrated in real time.

**P2: Spatial Coke Segregation**  
MCM-22 coke should preferentially deposit in supercages and external pockets (where F fires), not sinusoidal channels (where K blocks access). ZSM-5 coke should be pore-mouth localized. Testable by SEM/EDX or selective supercage blockade.

**P3: Acid-Site Relocation Phenotype Shift**  
Moving Brønsted sites from MCM-22 supercages into sinusoidal channels (C→K→F→U reordering) should shift selectivity toward ZSM-5-like behavior: suppressed coke, reduced aromatics, linear products only.
