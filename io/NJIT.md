FACILITIES, EQUIPMENT, & OTHER RESOURCES

1. FACILITIES
A. Computational & Operational Base (G6 LLC – Newark, NJ)
The applicant, G6 LLC, is a Newark-based small business. To optimize capital efficiency and ensure absolute control over proprietary intellectual property, all Phase I research tasks will be executed entirely solo by the Principal Investigator, Pablo Grossi.
The operational base for this project is a secure computational facility located in Newark, NJ. This space is equipped with high-speed internet, dedicated server space, and localized backup systems required to safely execute and store complex mathematical models, formal verification scripts, and kinetic simulation codebases. No external physical laboratory space or university infrastructure is required for the computational scope of this Phase I project.
------------------------------
2. EQUIPMENT
Because the scope of this project focuses entirely on formalizing, machine-checking, and mathematically verifying the kinetic and catalytic upgrading pathways, no physical laboratory hardware or analytical chemical instrumentation is required or requested.
G6 LLC possesses all necessary hardware infrastructure to execute the project tasks, including:

* High-Performance Workstations: Multi-core processing units optimized for heavy computational workloads, local symbolic execution, and algorithmic processing.
* Storage & Version Control: Encrypted, redundant solid-state storage arrays paired with secure, private Git-based version control frameworks to track formal proof developments.
* Development Environments: Integrated toolchains for formal methods, theorem proving, and machine-checking software architectures.

The core research builds directly upon the foundational, open-source verification work developed by the Principal Investigator, accessible via the public research repository at totogt.github.io/io. Phase II - FACILITIES, EQUIPMENT, & OTHER RESOURCES
1. FACILITIES
A. Commercial / Laboratory Space (VentureLink at NJIT – Newark, NJ)
The applicant, G6 LLC, is a registered New Jersey small business headquartered in Newark, NJ. To maximize capital efficiency during Phase I, the company utilizes a "virtual/fabless" operational model led by its Principal Investigator, Pablo Grossi. Physical laboratory tasks requiring wet-lab synthesis, chemical handling, and analytical characterization will be performed at VentureLink, New Jersey’s largest technology incubator, located on the campus of the New Jersey Institute of Technology (NJIT) in Newark, NJ.
Upon notification of award, G6 LLC will secure a dedicated wet-lab bench membership. This turnkey facility provides full environmental health and safety (EHS) compliance, chemical fume hoods, hazardous chemical waste management, climate-controlled storage, and utilities necessary to safely conduct ethanol-to-hydrocarbon catalytic upgrading experiments.
B. Computational / Office Space
Administrative, project management, and kinetic/molecular modeling tasks will be conducted using G6 LLC’s secure, proprietary computational infrastructure located in Newark, NJ. This office space is fully equipped with high-speed internet, secure data servers, and the necessary software suites for managing international collaborator workflows.
C. Foreign Collaborative Facilities (Brazilian University Network)
Note: Subject to DOE Foreign Work Waiver Approval.
Secondary, specialized catalyst characterization and regional feedstock validation will be sub-contracted to our academic partners in Brazil. These foreign academic facilities house unique, pilot-scale biomass/ethanol processing rigs and specialized high-throughput reactor systems tailored specifically to the regional Brazilian ethanol ecosystem.
------------------------------
2. EQUIPMENT
Through the VentureLink incubator membership and reciprocal user-facility access agreements with the New Jersey Institute of Technology (NJIT), G6 LLC will have shared, immediate access to a comprehensive suite of advanced chemical synthesis and analytical equipment. No major capital equipment purchases are requested in this Phase I budget.
Available analytical and synthesis equipment includes:

* Chromatography Systems: Gas Chromatography-Mass Spectrometry (GC-MS) and High-Performance Liquid Chromatography (HPLC) for precise quantification of ethanol-to-hydrocarbon conversion rates and product selectivity.
* Thermal & Surface Analysis: Fourier-Transform Infrared Spectroscopy (FTIR), Thermo-Gravimetric Analysis (TGA), and access to X-ray Diffraction (XRD) for post-reaction catalyst characterization and coking/deactivation analysis.
* Synthesis Infrastructure: Multi-position magnetic stirring hotplates, vacuum ovens, calcination furnaces, and analytical microbalances for precise catalyst formulation.
* Safety & Handling: Certified chemical fume hoods, localized gas lines (Nitrogen/Hydrogen), personal protective equipment (PPE), and specialized chemical storage cabinets.
